Lectures

Lectures are a key feature of university teaching. In broad terms, the principal purpose of a lecture is to provide you with information in an efficient, well-structured way. More particularly, lectures:

Before the lecture

  • Be on time for the lecture.
  • Complete any required reading (including handouts of lecture summaries) before you attend the lecture.
  • Ensure that you have sufficient paper, pens/pencils for note taking or any other equipment that you may need.

During the lecture

  • Decide what notes to write down, in how much detail to some extent.
  • The amount of details that will be included, depends on whether the information is relevant.
  • Note layout that you use will also determine the amount of detail you include.

After the lecture

  • Review your notes, this will help you recall the lecture.
  • Identify any gaps in your notes and add anything that is needed.
  • Store your notes in a loose-leaf folder, or a two ring binder. This will allow you to move pages around.

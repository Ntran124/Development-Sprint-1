Lectures

Practicals provide the opportunity for students to observe and manipulate materials and give insight into scientific methods and processes as well as scientific knowledge.

Before the practical, complete any required reading and ensure you have any necessary equipment necessary. During the practical, ensure that you listen to and follow the lecturerâ€™s instructions. Your notes should cover:

Notes to consider

  • Aim of the practical.
  • Safety Instructions
  • Details about the equipments & materials
  • Details about the method
  • Results of the exercise/experiment.
  • Important incidental suggestions made by the demonstrator
  • Important references that are relevant for the exercise/experiment.

During the lecture

  • Decide what notes to write down, in how much detail to some extent.
  • The amount of details that will be included, depends on whether the information is relevant.
  • Note layout that you use will also determine the amount of detail you include.

After the lecture

  • Review your notes, this will help you recall the lecture.
  • Identify any gaps in your notes and add anything that is needed.
  • Store your notes in a loose-leaf folder, or a two ring binder. This will allow you to move pages around.

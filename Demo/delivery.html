Study Skills: Lectures & Tutorials

Delivery

When giving a presentation, it can be nerve racking. So you must deliver your presentation to the best of your ability, this page focuses on how you should deliver your oral or seminar papaer presentation.

Before Presentation

  • Practice relevant terminology, concepts and words.
  • Practice your talk to a friend, or in front of a mirror to yourself.
  • Anticipate and prepare for audience questions
  • practice using any technical equipment you plan to use.

During Presentation

  • Maintain eye contact with your audience.
  • Pace your delivery so the presentation is neither rushed nor too drawn out.
  • Allow pauses, to add emphasis to your points or to provide dramatic effect.
  • Vary the tone of your voice to keep the audienceâ€™s interest.

Visual Aids

  • Well designed and appropriate for your audience.
  • Relevant to the presentation.
  • Well integrated into the presentation.

Responding to Questions

  • Listen carefully to the questioner and answer the question as fully as possible.
  • If you didn't understand the question, ask the questioner to rephrase it.
  • Address your answers to the whole audience.
